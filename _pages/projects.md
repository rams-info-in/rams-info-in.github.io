=>**Illuminating the antimicrobial activities of small molecules present in Abutilon theophrasti against drug resistant Neisseria gonorrhoeae: An in-silico approach.**
•	Targeted the unexplored virulent FtsY protein of Neisseria gonorrhoeae to combat multi-drug resistance, modeled its 3D structure via homology modelling and validated using structural analysis tools due to the absence of an experimentally driven crystallographic structure. Virtual screening phytochemical database created from Abutilon theophrasti and evaluated.
•	Identified Chrysoeriol as a top candidate with favorable pharmacokinetics and a strong binding affinity (−6.9 kcal/mol), significantly outperforming the control antibiotic ceftriaxone’s binding potential with its target.
